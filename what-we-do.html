Meet Dr Nyoka

I grew up in the Red Location, a township near Port Elizabeth in the Eastern Cape
where my father had a medical practice for over 50 years. Red Location is the 2nd
oldest black township in South Africa. It, like many other black communities, is
plagued by poverty and has an unemployment rate in excess of 40%.

The name “RED” stems from the colour of the zinc with which the early dwellings
in the area were built. This was material was exported from the concentration
camps of the Anglo-Boer war. The Red Location, like most of province, the
Eastern Cape, which has produced champions such as Makhaya Ntini and Siya
Kolisi, has a dire shortage of sports facilities. The children of the community have
thus little recreationally to do.

While the past, with all its oppressive lineaments, is unfortunately irrevocable, a
new and better future can be created. During my time as President of Cricket
South Africa and the Africa Cricket association, I witnessed the transformative
power of sport on individuals, communities and on nations. None of the
successful and thriving countries I visited didn’t have a very strong and pervasive
culture of sport. Our goal therefore is to use sport as a tool to uplift and free the
children in the Red Location from the shackles of their country’s past.

Meet Ayabonga Ngqono

Where I come from:
I was born and bred in Butterworth Cape Town, but have lived in Gqeberha (P.E.)
since I obtained my law degree from Nelson Mandela University.

What I do:
My first company was in fashion – Ayanna Fashion Cabin. From there I went on to
work at the Master’s office where I learned how to administer deceased and
living estates. After that I was a candidate attorney at Mbewana Attorneys –
the largest, black-owned law firm in Gqeberha (P.E.).

Why I am a R.E.D. Ambassador:
I am now a Director at Acketso Legal House. It is here that I started to champion
different causes that give back to my community. I draft free wills for the elderly.

I also sponsor and coach the St George’s football team. I am especially proud of
the fact that the boys do not just go home with improved soccer skills but also a
full tummy. I am passionate about giving back to the community and am
honoured to be a R.E.D Ambassador.

Meet Hintsa Mpe

Where I come from:
I was born in New Brighton. My humble beginnings played a part in the person
who I am today.

What I do:
I am proud to say that I am an entrepreneur and the Director of Mthi Wembotyi
Projects, a company that operates in the marine industry in the ports of Coega
and Gqeberha.

I have been recognised by the South African Maritime Safety Authority as the
first black-owned SMME business to operate within the marine sector in
Gqeberha.

Why I am a R.E.D. Ambassador: The name of my company, ‘Mthi Wembotyi’, is a strong tree that that carries
seeds in large pods. The seeds symbolise hope and courage. My passion is to
bring hope and courage to the youth in the Red Location – that’s why I am
a R.E.D. Ambassador.
